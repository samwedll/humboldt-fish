# Gear Recommendations by Trip Type

Reference for what to bring based on target species and conditions. Always-required gear is listed once at the top; species-specific blocks follow.

---

## ALWAYS-REQUIRED (every trip, no exceptions)

### Safety (non-negotiable)
- [ ] **Immersion protection** — semi-dry paddling top + dry pants + 3mm long john + neoprene gloves + neoprene boots + beanie. Verify all gaskets before launch. Humboldt water is 50-55°F year-round.
- [ ] **PFD** — fishing-cut Type III, high-back ✅ owned
- [ ] **VHF radio** — handheld with DSC, registered MMSI, tested before launch on Ch 16
- [ ] **PLB** — registered with NOAA at beaconregistration.noaa.gov
- [ ] **Helmet** if launching through surf (not needed for protected launches)
- [ ] **Knife** — accessible, blunt-tip, secured to PFD ✅ owned
- [ ] **Whistle, signal mirror, strobe** — all PFD-mounted ✅ owned
- [ ] **Headlamp + spare** — even for daytime trips, in case of unexpected delay
- [ ] **Phone in waterproof case** — accessible, not in dry hatch
- [ ] **Compass** (deck-mounted or wrist) — fog navigation backup
- [ ] **First aid kit** (basic — bleeding control, hooks-removal scissors, fishhook out tool)
- [ ] **Visibility flag** — Hobie Safety Flag Light Combo ✅ owned (came with boat)

### Float plan
- [ ] **Float plan filed with the designated shore contact** before leaving: launch location, launch time, target return time, vehicle, area to be fished
- [ ] **Check-in time agreed** — if no contact by [time + 1 hr], call USCG Station Humboldt Bay (verify number 707-839-6113 currency)
- [ ] **Template lives in `context.md`** under "Float Plan Template"

### Boat & paddling
- [ ] Paddle + spare paddle (split paddle on deck)
- [ ] Bilge pump
- [ ] Paddle leash
- [ ] Tow rope (15-20 ft)
- [ ] Kayak cart (for ramp-to-water if launch ramp is rough)
- [ ] Hatch covers all secured

### Fishing license/cards
- [ ] Current CA sport fishing license (in waterproof license holder)
- [ ] Ocean Enhancement Validation (if fishing south of Pt. Arguello — not required for Humboldt but no harm)
- [ ] Halibut report card (if targeting halibut)
- [ ] Steelhead report card (if targeting steelhead in rivers)

### Hydration & nutrition
- [ ] At least 1 L water (2 L for trips >3 hr)
- [ ] Snacks (something not soaked-by-saltwater proof — sealed bars work)

## ROCKFISH / LINGCOD

### Rod & reel
- [ ] 1-2 conventional setups: 7-7'6" medium-heavy rod, conventional reel with line counter (Shimano Tekota 500 or Daiwa Saltist 30LWLD class)
- [ ] 40-60 lb braid main line, 30-40 lb mono/fluoro leader

### Terminal tackle
- [ ] Sliding sinker rigs with 6-12 oz weights (depth-dependent — go heavier in current)
- [ ] Shrimp flies or rockfish jigs (white, chartreuse, blue/silver)
- [ ] Swimbaits 4-6" (Big Hammer, Berkley Gulp, MC Slug)
- [ ] Live bait sabiki rig if catching anchovies/smelt for lingcod
- [ ] Jig heads 1-3 oz to match swimbaits
- [ ] Circle hooks 4/0-7/0 for live bait

### Specific to rockfish/lingcod
- [ ] **Descending device** (SeaQualizer, Shelton, weighted milk crate) — REQUIRED by law for federal groundfish from a boat
- [ ] Fish gripper / lip grip (lings have teeth)
- [ ] Stringer or fish bag (insulated kayak fish bag if going long)
- [ ] **Measuring device** — sticker on kayak deck or rigid measuring board (lingcod must be 22" total length)

### Reference
- See `regs/rockfish-lingcod-2026-northern.md` for limits and seasons
- Northern Mgmt Area: 10 RCG aggregate, 1 copper, 4 vermilion/sunset, 2 canary; 2 lingcod at 22"

## SALMON

### Rod & reel
- [ ] 7'6"-8'6" medium-action trolling rod with parabolic bend
- [ ] Line-counter reel (same Shimano Tekota or Daiwa as rockfish setup — can repurpose with leader change)
- [ ] 30 lb braid main, 20-30 lb mono/fluoro leader

### Trolling tackle
- [ ] Downrigger OR diving plane (Deep Six or Dipsy Diver) — kayak-mountable Cannon Mini-Mag or Scotty 1106 if downrigger
- [ ] Flashers / dodgers (Pro-Troll Hot Chip, Hot Spot — bring 2-3 colors)
- [ ] Hoochies / squid skirts (multiple colors)
- [ ] Anchovy holders / bait threads
- [ ] Frozen anchovies (or live if you can sabiki them up)
- [ ] Spoons (Coyote, Krippled K)

### Specific to salmon
- [ ] **Two single-point single-shank barbless hooks max** (federal/state requirement)
- [ ] **One rod per angler** (federal/state requirement)
- [ ] Net (long-handle landing net, kayak-friendly)
- [ ] Priest / fish bonker (humane dispatch)
- [ ] Larger fish bag (king salmon can run 15-25+ lb)
- [ ] **Phone the (707) 576-3429 hotline before launch** — confirm season open

### Reference
- See `regs/salmon-2026-kmz.md` for season dates and bag limits
- KMZ 2026: open Jun 13 - Jul 19 and Aug 1 - Aug 31, 2 Chinook/day, 20" min, no coho

## HALIBUT (Pacific halibut, in-bay or outside)

### Rod & reel
- [ ] Heavier conventional setup: 6'6"-7' medium-heavy rod, larger reel
- [ ] 50-65 lb braid

### Tackle
- [ ] Spreader bar rigs or sliding sinker rigs with 12-16 oz weights
- [ ] Live bait (anchovies, smelt) or large swimbaits
- [ ] Big circle hooks 6/0-9/0
- [ ] Halibut harpoon or sturdy gaff (legal-size halibut are big and strong)

### Specific to halibut
- [ ] **Halibut report card** (required, must be filled out at landing)
- [ ] Pen for filling out the card
- [ ] Larger cooler / fish bag (legal halibut are 32"+ in CA generally — verify current limits before season)

## DUNGENESS CRAB

### Gear
- [ ] **Crab ring or hoop net** ✅ owned (crab ring)
- [ ] **Crab trap with line and float** (if using traps — different from rings)
- [ ] **Bait** — chicken parts, frozen fish carcasses, or commercial crab bait
- [ ] **Bait cage / bait sock** to keep bait in the trap
- [ ] **Crab gauge / measuring tool** — males must be 5¾" carapace width (Pacific) — verify current rules
- [ ] **Cooler with ice** for keeping crab cool on the way home

### Specific to crab
- [ ] **Crab Trap Validation** ✅ owned (purchased with 2026 license)
- [ ] **Sport crab regulations** — generally Nov 1 to Jul 30 north of Pt. Arena (verify current; check CDFW)
- [ ] **Bag limit:** 10 Dungeness per day (verify current rules)
- [ ] **Trap tag with the user's name and address** — required on all traps

## SURFPERCH / JACKSMELT (bay or beach launches)

### Rod & reel
- [ ] Lighter setup: 7-9' medium spinning rod, 3000-4000 size spinning reel
- [ ] 10-15 lb braid main, 8-12 lb fluoro leader

### Tackle
- [ ] Carolina rig or high-low rig
- [ ] Sand crab imitations (Berkley Gulp Sandworms, sand fleas)
- [ ] Small hooks (size 4-6)
- [ ] 1-3 oz pyramid sinkers

### Specific
- [ ] Sand spike if launching from beach
- [ ] Bucket / stringer

## CONDITIONS-DEPENDENT ADD-ONS

### Cold day (air <50°F or wind chill issue)
- [ ] Insulating layer under drysuit (fleece base + mid layer)
- [ ] Pogies or neoprene gloves
- [ ] Skull cap or beanie
- [ ] Hot drink in thermos

### Hot day (rare in Humboldt but happens)
- [ ] Wide-brim sun hat
- [ ] Sunscreen (reef-safe)
- [ ] Polarized sunglasses + retainer
- [ ] Extra water (2L+ for any trip >2 hr)

### Foggy day (any visibility concern)
- [ ] White stern light + masthead light if launching pre-civil-twilight
- [ ] VHF on Ch 16 the entire trip
- [ ] Air horn or other sound signal
- [ ] Compass mounted on deck
- [ ] GPS or phone with offline charts (Navionics or similar)
- [ ] Confirm float plan is filed and check-in time set

### Predawn launch
- [ ] White stern light (360° visible) — required by USCG for kayaks at night
- [ ] Headlamp with red filter mode
- [ ] Backup light source

### Salmon-specific add-on for KMZ trips
- [ ] **CDFW salmon hotline phone number programmed:** (707) 576-3429
- [ ] Verify call made within 2 hours of launch

## KAYAK-SPECIFIC PACKING NOTES

### Where things go
- **PFD pockets:** Knife, whistle, VHF (clipped), small first aid, snacks
- **Deck:** Paddle leash, spare paddle (split), tow rope (front bag), measuring sticker, descending device
- **Front hatch:** Dry clothes for emergency, food, water
- **Rear hatch:** Fish bag, extra tackle, spare line
- **Center console / cockpit:** Tackle for active use, rods, fish gripper, net

### Pre-launch check (run through this every time)
1. Drysuit on, properly sealed at neck/wrists/ankles
2. PFD on with all gear
3. VHF on Ch 16 — **test transmit with float plan contact**
4. Phone in waterproof case, accessible
5. Hatches all sealed
6. Paddle leash attached
7. Float plan confirmed
8. License and any required cards in waterproof holder
9. Bar status verified (if outside-jetty trip)
10. Salmon hotline called (if salmon trip)

## What NOT to bring (or stop bringing)

- Cotton anything — kills you when wet
- Rigid containers without straps (will float away in a swim)
- Loose tackle boxes — switch to side-locking Plano boxes
- Long fillet knives in unsecured sheaths
- A second rod you'll never deploy — clutters the kayak
