## Humboldt-Specific Operations

### Launch Sites (skill progression order)

#### Tier 1 — Skill-building (start here)
- **Freshwater Lagoon** — closed water, no tide, no current, easy ramp
- **Big Lagoon** — brackish, sheltered, paved boat ramp; tide doesn't matter (sandbar usually closed)
- **Stone Lagoon** — similar profile to Big Lagoon
- **Mad River Slough** — tidal but sheltered; ride flood in, ebb out

#### Tier 2 — Humboldt Bay
- **Samoa Boat Ramp County Park** — best primary launch, paved double-lane, parking, restrooms
- **Fields Landing Boat Ramp** — South Bay, less crowded
- **Woodley Island Marina** — Humboats nearby; dock launches; Tuluwat egret rookery loop
- **Eureka Public Marina** — two-lane ramp, fish cleaning station

#### Tier 3 — Trinidad Harbor
- **Indian Beach launch** — sandy beach in Trinidad State Beach lot (don't use Seascape Restaurant lot)
- Sheltered in NW weather, dangerous in W/S storms
- **Kayak Trinidad** on Scenic Drive — local Hobie dealer for parts/service
- Trinidad Harbor monitors VHF Channel 78

#### Tier 4 — Open Pacific
- **North/South Jetties at Humboldt Bay entrance** — only on calm bar days
- **Eel River Estuary** (Cannibal Rd, Loleta) — sheltered ocean staging
- **Pedrazzini Boat Ramp** at Eel mouth

### Tide Considerations

- **Humboldt Bay swings 5-8 ft** between high and low — currents 3-5 knots at entrance
- **Lagoons (Freshwater, Big, Stone): tide doesn't matter** — closed or barely tidal
- **Bay rules:** Launch on last 2 hours of incoming, fish at slack high, return on first 2 hours of outgoing
- **Tide tools:** Tides Near Me app, NOAA station 9418767 (North Spit), Tide-Forecast.com
- **Never get caught at low tide** in mudflat areas of bay — boat sits on mud watching launch from across

### Weather Windows

- **Launch early** — wind picks up by 11 AM consistently
- **Check NOAA Eureka marine forecast** night before and morning of
- **Check Humboldt Bar Report** if crossing bay entrance — VHF 22A or USCG
- **Cancel if:** sustained wind 15+ kts, swell 6+ ft short period, dense fog, small craft advisory

### Marine Protected Areas (MPAs)

- **300-ft Special Closure zones** around offshore rocks — fishing inside is illegal
- **Maps at wildlife.ca.gov** — review before fishing new areas
- **Tribal co-management areas** — additional restrictions in some zones

### Critical Phone Numbers

- **USCG Station Humboldt Bay:** 707-839-6113 (verify before relying on it)
- **USCG VHF emergency:** Channel 16
- **USCG Bar Report:** VHF Channel 22A
- **Trinidad Harbor monitoring:** VHF Channel 78
- **CDFW Ocean Salmon Hotline:** 707-576-3429
- **NMFS Federal Regulations:** 800-662-9825

## Key Skill Notes for Saltwater Transition

The user is an experienced freshwater paddler. The transition skills to develop:

1. **MirageDrive operation** — pedal feel, instant reverse lever, deployment in skinny water
2. **Saltwater dynamics** — chop and swell behave differently than river current or lake wind
3. **Tide reading** — invisible until it isn't; plan for the return, not the launch
4. **PA-14 specific handling** — wide and stable, very different from narrow paddling kayaks
5. **Pacific cold-water risk** — 50-55°F year-round; flip means real consequences
6. **Wet exit + re-entry with drive deployed** — practice in shallow lagoon water before needing it
7. **Fishing-specific gear management** — rods, tackle, livewell handling on a kayak
8. **MPA awareness** — different from river/lake regulation patterns
