# Humboldt County Kayak Launches

Reference for matching conditions to launch site, organized by **skill-progression tier** (Tier 1 most protected, Tier 4 most exposed). the user should generally work up the tiers — but conditions can also force a tier-down on any given day.

---

## ⚠️ User Personal Rules (override skill defaults)

**No Humboldt Bay jetty crossings — ever.** the user has substantial powerboat experience through the Humboldt entrance and considers the bar too dangerous to cross in a kayak under any conditions. This rule is permanent and is not unlocked by good bar reports, calm days, or future ocean experience.

**Trinidad is the only open-Pacific launch.** Open-ocean fishing is restricted to Trinidad. Other open-ocean options in this file (Eel River past the mouth, Pedrazzini past the mouth, Crescent City outside the breakwater, Shelter Cove) are personally ruled out and should not be recommended even if conditions are green.

**Bay launches remain fine for inside-bay fishing** — South Bay, North Bay channels, Arcata Bay flats, Woodley Island area. Just stay away from the entrance channel near the jetties.

These rules are user-set and should be applied first, before running the standard tier ladder. They turn several "not solo year 1" launches into "permanently off the table."

## Tier 1 — Skill-Building / Protected Water

These are go-anywhere-anytime launches. No tide concerns, no swell, no bar. Useful for MirageDrive practice, gear shakedowns, and "ocean is blown out but I still want to paddle" days.

### Freshwater Lagoon
- **Drive from Blue Lake:** ~30 min, ~22 mi north on 101
- **Launch:** Easy ramp on the north side
- **Conditions:** Closed water, no tide, no current
- **Target species:** Limited — bluegill, bass, occasional trout
- **Use case:** Paddle/pedal practice, gear testing, family days
- **Solo OK?** Yes

### Big Lagoon
- **Drive from Blue Lake:** ~25 min, ~20 mi
- **Launch:** Big Lagoon County Park paved ramp
- **Conditions:** Brackish, sheltered. Tide doesn't matter most of the year (sandbar usually closed). Wind exposure is the only real concern.
- **Target species:** Cutthroat trout (in season), perch occasionally
- **Use case:** When the ocean is unfishable but you want water time
- **Solo OK?** Yes

### Stone Lagoon
- **Drive from Blue Lake:** ~30 min
- Similar profile to Big Lagoon. Same use cases.

### Mad River Slough
- **Drive from Blue Lake:** ~10 min — closest to home
- **Conditions:** Tidal but sheltered. Mud bottom — don't get caught at low tide.
- **Strategy:** Ride the flood in, ebb out. Net flow over a trip should match the tide cycle.
- **Target species:** Striped bass occasionally, perch, jacksmelt
- **Use case:** Quick after-work paddles, MirageDrive in tidal current practice
- **Solo OK?** Yes

## Tier 2 — Humboldt Bay (inside the jetties)

Protected from ocean swell by the jetties and bar. Wind, tide currents, and mudflats are the concerns. **No bar crossing required.**

⚠️ **Jetty entrance channel is permanently off-limits per user rule** — fish the bay generally (channels, flats, sloughs, around Woodley/Tuluwat) but stay out of the entrance area where powerboat traffic, current, and the proximity to the bar create unnecessary risk.

### Samoa Boat Ramp County Park
- **Drive from Blue Lake:** ~25 min, ~17 mi
- **Launch:** Best primary bay launch — paved double-lane, ample parking, restrooms
- **Conditions:** Inside bay; tide currents up to 3-5 kt at the entrance channel; wind builds chop fast in the open bay
- **Target species:** Surfperch, jacksmelt, occasional Pacific halibut (in season), Dungeness crab via hoop net (in season)
- **Tide rule:** Launch on last 2 hours of incoming, fish at slack high, return on first 2 hours of outgoing. **Never get caught at low tide in mudflat areas.**
- **Solo OK?** Yes

### Fields Landing Boat Ramp
- **Drive from Blue Lake:** ~30 min
- **Launch:** South Bay, less crowded than Samoa
- **Conditions:** Same bay-inside profile, accesses south bay channels
- **Solo OK?** Yes

### Woodley Island Marina
- **Drive from Blue Lake:** ~25 min
- **Launch:** Dock launches (not ramp — different technique)
- **Conditions:** Right next to Eureka, near Hum-Boats for any rental/repair needs
- **Bonus:** Tuluwat Island egret rookery loop is a nice paddle even when not fishing
- **Solo OK?** Yes

### Eureka Public Marina
- **Drive from Blue Lake:** ~25 min
- **Launch:** Two-lane ramp, fish cleaning station on-site
- **Conditions:** Bay-inside; access to channels
- **Solo OK?** Yes

## Tier 3 — Trinidad Harbor

Direct ocean access but with significant wind/swell shadow from Trinidad Head. The most protected outside-jetty launch in the county.

**This is the user's only open-ocean launch.** Per the user personal rule at the top of this file, Trinidad is where ocean fishing happens — full stop. All Trinidad recommendations should assume this is the default open-water destination, not "one of several options."

### Indian Beach (Trinidad State Beach)
- **Drive from Blue Lake:** ~30 min, ~20 mi
- **Launch:** Sandy beach in the State Beach parking lot. **Don't use the Seascape Restaurant lot** — that's their parking, and they're particular about it.
- **Protection:** Trinidad Head provides major wind/swell shadow from N and NW. Dangerous in W or S storms when the headland doesn't shelter.
- **Target species:** Black rockfish, lingcod (close in around Trinidad Head), occasional cabezon, salmon possible nearshore (when in season)
- **Solo OK?** Inside the harbor area: yes. Around Trinidad Head into open ocean: not in year-1 ocean.
- **Bar/jetty notes:** No bar to cross — direct beach launch. **Trinidad Harbor monitors VHF Channel 78** for advisories.
- **Parking:** State Beach lot
- **Bathroom:** Yes

### Kayak Trinidad (resource, not launch)
- **Location:** Scenic Drive, Trinidad
- Local Hobie dealer for parts/service. Worth a relationship for MirageDrive servicing, fin replacement, etc.

## Tier 4 — Open Pacific (highest exposure)

These launches put you in full Pacific conditions. **All entries in this section are permanently ruled out per the user personal rule at the top of this file.** They remain documented here only for completeness — do NOT recommend them, even on green days. If a request would otherwise route here, route to Trinidad (Tier 3) instead, or no-go the day.

### ❌ North/South Jetties at Humboldt Bay Entrance — RULED OUT
- **Status:** Permanently excluded by user rule. the user knows the bar from years of powerboat use and will not cross it in a kayak.
- **Documentation only:** Samoa-side launch, paddle out the entrance channel, full Pacific exposure outside, bar crossing required.

### ❌ Eel River Estuary (Cannibal Rd, Loleta) — open ocean past the mouth RULED OUT
- **Drive from Blue Lake:** ~50 min south on 101
- **Estuary inside the mouth:** still permitted as a sheltered Tier-1-equivalent paddle. The estuary itself is not "open ocean."
- **Past the mouth:** ruled out — counts as open ocean and Trinidad is the only open-ocean launch.

### ❌ Pedrazzini Boat Ramp (Eel River mouth) — open ocean past the mouth RULED OUT
- Same logic as Eel River Estuary above. Inside the river/estuary OK; past the mouth ruled out.

### ❌ Crescent City Harbor (outside breakwater) — RULED OUT
- **Inside breakwater:** still permitted (Tier-2-equivalent harbor paddle). Documented in the "Notes" section below if Crescent City inside-fishing comes up.
- **Outside breakwater:** ruled out — not Trinidad.

### ❌ Shelter Cove — RULED OUT
- Long drive, remote, fog-prone road, and not Trinidad. Permanently off the table.

## Launch Selection Decision Tree

```
Step 0: Apply user personal rules (always first)
  Open ocean target?           → Trinidad only. Tier 4 entries are off the table.
  Want to fish near Humboldt jetty entrance?  → No. Stay in bay interior.

Step 1: Wind
  >15 kt sustained or gusts?   → Tier 1 only (lagoons, slough)
  10-15 kt sustained?          → Tier 1-2 (lagoons, bay inside)
  <10 kt sustained?            → Continue.

Step 2: Swell (only matters if going to Trinidad)
  >5 ft OR period <10 sec?     → Tier 1-2 only (no Trinidad today)
  Aligned w/ wind, ≤5 ft, ≥10 sec? → Trinidad on the table.

Step 3: Solo?
  Yes  → Tier 1-2 + Trinidad inside the cove. (Trinidad Head outside the cove is still not solo year 1.)
  No   → Same set, plus Trinidad Head outside on green days.

Step 4: Target species drives final selection
  Salmon (when open)   → Trinidad nearshore (within the user's range from Indian Beach)
  Rockfish/Lingcod     → Trinidad — close in around Trinidad Head structure
  Pacific halibut      → Off the table for the user given Trinidad-only rule (deeper water needed)
  CA halibut           → Tier 2 inside the bay (away from jetty entrance)
  Crab                 → Tier 2 (bay) or Tier 3 (Trinidad)
  Surfperch/Jacksmelt  → Tier 1-2 (slough, bay, beach)
  Cutthroat            → Tier 1 lagoons
```

## Backup launch ladder by primary species

If primary plan fails the conditions check, here's the fallback:

- **Salmon (Trinidad needed)** → If Trinidad fails the conditions check, no salmon today. Bay/lagoon won't have salmon.
- **Lingcod/rockfish** → Trinidad outside the cove (good days) → Trinidad inside the cove (limited but possible) → Bay inside (different species but on water)
- **Pacific halibut** → Generally off-target for the user given the Trinidad-only rule (Pacific halibut hold over deeper sand farther offshore). CA halibut inside the bay (T2) is the realistic alternative.
- **Crab** → Bay (T2, away from entrance) always works in any non-gale conditions
- **Surfperch / panfish / fun paddle** → Tier 1 always works
