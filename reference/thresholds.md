# Personal Go/No-Go Thresholds

**Calibrated for:** the user — experienced freshwater paddler, first ocean kayak fishing season, a specific 14-foot pedal kayak (38" beam, 600 lb capacity), specific semi-dry layering system, fishing from Humboldt County.
**Last updated:** May 2, 2026
**Status:** Conservative defaults for ocean exposure. Looser for protected water given existing paddling skill. Edit as ocean experience grows.

---

## Hard No-Go Conditions (any single fail = no-go)

| Factor | Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Sustained wind at launch time** | ≤ 10 kt | NOAA forecast or buoy reading at hour of launch |
| **Sustained wind during trip window** | ≤ 15 kt | Forecast for full duration on the water |
| **Wind gusts** | ≤ 15 kt | Gust factor matters more than sustained — 10G20 days are deceiving |
| **Swell height** | ≤ 5 ft | Significant wave height from buoy or forecast |
| **Swell period** | ≥ 10 sec | Short-period swell breaks earlier and steeper |
| **Wind direction vs swell direction** | Aligned within 45° | Opposing wind/swell creates dangerous chop |
| **USCG Humboldt Bar advisory** | No restriction | Any "restricted" or "closed" status = no-go for kayak |
| **Visibility forecast** | ≥ 1 nm | Fog moves in fast on the North Coast |
| **Water temperature** | semi-dry layering required <60°F | Always required in Humboldt — ocean is consistently 50-55°F. Layering = semi-dry paddling top + dry pants + 3mm long john + neoprene gloves + booties + beanie. Verify all gaskets before launch. |

## Bar Crossing Rules (Humboldt Bay specifically)

- **Cross only on:** Slack water or flood tide.
- **Never cross on:** Ebb tide, especially with any west swell or onshore wind.
- **Bar status check:** USCG Sector Humboldt Bay bar advisory must be "Open / No Restrictions" within 2 hours of crossing.
- **Backup plan if conditions change while outside:** Wait inside the jetty line until conditions improve, or land on the inside (Samoa beach) and walk back. Do not attempt to cross a restricted bar in a kayak.

## Solo vs. Accompanied

Note: The semi-dry layering system (semi-dry top + dry pants + 3mm long john) provides ~80-90% of drysuit immersion protection. This is fine for protected water (lagoons, bay) and shorter-duration outside-jetty trips, but means time-in-water tolerance is reduced compared to a full drysuit. Outside-jetty solo trips year 1 are no-go regardless.

## Predawn / Low-Light Launch Rules

- OK to launch in pre-dawn IF:
  - All other thresholds are met
  - Nav lights mounted and tested
  - Float plan filed with the designated shore contact
  - Phone charged + waterproof + accessible (not buried in dry hatch)
  - VHF on and tested before leaving the ramp
- Not OK to launch in pre-dawn IF:
  - Any threshold above is marginal
  - Solo on outside-jetty water (regardless of other conditions)

## Trip Duration

- **Year 1 default cap:** 4 hours on the water from launch to land.
- Longer trips require all green and a check-in plan with shore contact.

## When to upgrade thresholds

Wait until you have at least 10 successful trips matching current thresholds before relaxing any single number. The Pacific doesn't reward overconfidence.

Specific upgrade path I'd suggest reviewing after a season:
- Wind sustained: 10 kt → 12 kt
- Swell: 5 ft → 6 ft (only if period stays ≥10 sec)
- Solo outside jetties: still no until at least year 2

Do not upgrade:
- Bar advisory tolerance (always no-go on restricted)
- Drysuit requirement (always required)
- Wind/swell opposing rule (always no-go)
