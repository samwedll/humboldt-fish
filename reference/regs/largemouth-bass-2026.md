# Largemouth Bass — California Inland Waters 2026

**Last updated:** 2026-05-17 (DRAFT — verify with CDFW before relying on)
**Region:** California freshwater; specifically the user's Humboldt-area inland lakes
**Source for verification:** <https://wildlife.ca.gov/Fishing/Inland/Black-Bass>

---

## Season

**Open year-round** in most California inland waters. Some specific waters have closures during spawning (typically March–June); verify per-water before trips.

## Bag limits

- **Daily bag:** 5 fish total in the black-bass family (largemouth, smallmouth, spotted combined)
- **Possession:** 10 fish (2× daily)

## Size limits

- **Minimum:** 12 inches total length

## License and stamps

- **Required:** CDFW Sport Fishing License (age 16+)
- **No special black-bass stamp** required for sport fishing

## Gear and technique

- Medium-heavy spinning or baitcasting rod, 10-15 lb test
- Soft plastic worms (Texas-rigged, drop-shot, weightless), spinnerbaits, crankbaits, swimbaits
- Topwater early/late on calm days
- Catch-and-release encouraged for larger fish; bass populations in small lagoons are easily overharvested

## Launch suitability

- **Freshwater Lagoon** — primary largemouth target in the user's launch set; healthy population per local reports
- Not viable at the brackish lagoons (Big / Stone — too saline for largemouth)
- Not viable at saltwater launches
