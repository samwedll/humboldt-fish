# 2026 Recreational Ocean Salmon — California KMZ

**Zone:** OR/CA border (42°00' N) to 40°10' N latitude (just south of Cape Mendocino)
**Applies to:** Crescent City, Trinidad, Humboldt Bay, Eureka launches — anywhere north of Shelter Cove
**Last updated:** May 2, 2026
**Source:** PFMC final adoption April 7-12, 2026; CDFW Marine Management News April 13, 2026

---

## ⚠️ VERIFY BEFORE EVERY TRIP

This is a **quota-managed in-season fishery**. Open dates can close early without warning when the harvest guideline is hit.

- **CDFW Ocean Salmon Regulations Hotline: (707) 576-3429**
- NMFS West Coast hotline: (800) 662-9825 or (206) 526-6667
- CDFW Ocean Salmon page: <https://wildlife.ca.gov/Fishing/Ocean/Regulations/Salmon>

Call the hotline the morning of every salmon trip. The 2025 season closed after 2 days when the quota hit. Don't assume.

## Quick Reference

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| **Spring/summer open dates** | June 13 – July 19, then Aug 1 – Aug 31 |
| **Fall season** | Begins September (20,000 Chinook fall guideline statewide) |
| **Harvest guideline (KMZ spring/summer)** | 3,900 Chinook |
| **Bag limit** | 2 Chinook per day |
| **Possession limit** | 2 daily bag limits (verify in regs booklet) |
| **Minimum size** | 20" total length (verify with hotline before first trip) |
| **Coho** | **Prohibited** — must release |
| **Hooks** | Max 2 single-point, single-shank, barbless hooks |
| **Rods** | One rod per angler when fishing for salmon or on a boat with salmon aboard |
| **Days per week** | Open seven days per week during open dates |

**68 total open days projected for the KMZ in 2026** — the longest season in three years.

## Klamath Control Zone (special closure inside KMZ)

Even when KMZ is open, a small area at the Klamath River mouth may be closed:

- **Bounded by:** 41°38'48" N (north), 124°23'00" W (west, ~12 nm offshore), 41°26'48" N (south)
- This is approximately a 12 nm wide x 6 nm tall box centered on the Klamath River mouth
- **Check status before fishing this area** — closures vary by inseason action

For a kayaker out of Trinidad or Humboldt Bay this is well north of typical range, but worth knowing if you ever launch from Crescent City or fish near the Klamath.

---

## Gear Rules (federal + state, applicable in KMZ)

- **Hook count:** No more than 2 single-point, single-shank barbless hooks
- **Rod count:** One rod per angler when targeting salmon OR on any vessel with salmon on board
- **Circle hook definition (CCR T-14, §182):** A hook with a generally circular shape and a point that turns inwards, pointing directly to the shank
- **Trolling definition:** Angling from a boat or floating device making way under power, other than drifting on current/weather
- **Transit with salmon aboard:** Unlawful to have fishing gear in the water while transiting any area closed to salmon fishing while possessing salmon (CCR T-14, §182)

## Required Documentation

- Current California sport fishing license (16+)
- **No salmon report card required** for ocean salmon (unlike river salmon — the Klamath/Trinity river fisheries DO require a Klamath River Basin Steelhead Report Card / specific in-river requirements)

---

## Why 2026 is the First Real Season Since 2022

- 2023 closed (rec + commercial)
- 2024 closed (rec + commercial)
- 2025: 2-day rec opener only, commercial closed
- **2026:** ~68 KMZ rec days driven by ~176,000 Klamath fall Chinook forecast and ~392,000 Sacramento fall Chinook — both more than double 2025's projections.

This is a meaningful first-year-back season but **the inseason management framework is the same**. Quotas are tracked near-real-time and seasons close when hit.

---

## Fall Season (September onward)

- A second fall salmon season begins in September with a **20,000 Chinook fall harvest guideline applicable to all open areas statewide**
- Specific KMZ fall dates are subject to the same inseason management process
- **Check CDFW Ocean Salmon page in late August** for fall opener details

---

## Practical Notes for Kayak Fishing from Humboldt

- Boats out of Trinidad and Eureka will be on the salmon immediately when KMZ opens June 13. Expect crowded ramps the first weekend.
- A kayak has access to the same productive waters as power boats for nearshore salmon — often closer to shore is fine, particularly off the Trinidad area
- **Check local tide and bar conditions** — Humboldt Bar has killed people. Don't cross on an ebb against a swell.
- The PFMC manages this under the Magnuson-Stevens Act; federal in-season actions show up at NOAA Fisheries' bulletin page: <https://www.fisheries.noaa.gov/bulletin/series/inseason-action-notice-pacific-salmon>
