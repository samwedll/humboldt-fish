# Dungeness Crab (Sport) — California 2026

**Last updated:** 2026-05-17 (DRAFT — verify with CDFW + CDPH before relying on)
**Region:** Humboldt Bay, Mad River Slough, all California marine waters north of Pt. Arguello
**Sources for verification:**
- CDFW: <https://wildlife.ca.gov/Fishing/Ocean/Regulations/Crab>
- CDPH domoic-acid advisories: <https://www.cdph.ca.gov/Programs/CEH/DEODC/EHIB/EMB/Pages/MarineBiotoxinMonitoring.aspx>

## Season

- **Sport season:** Nov 1 – July 30 (historical)
- **Off-season:** Aug 1 – Oct 31 (closed)

Inseason closures **routinely happen** due to domoic acid (DA) toxicity in crab meat. CDFW + CDPH issue health advisories that can close specific zones inseason. **Always check the CDPH advisory page before launching for crab.**

## Bag limits

- **Daily bag:** 10 crabs

## Size limits

- **Minimum:** 5.75 inches measured across the widest part of the back (carapace), in front of (not including) the points

## License and stamps

- **Required:** CDFW Sport Fishing License (16+)
- **No special crab stamp** required for sport
- **No commercial permit** unless selling

## Gear and technique

- **Hoop net** is the standard kayak gear: ~30" hoop, weighted bait box, 100+ ft of line, buoy on top
- Fresh bait: chicken parts, fish racks (rockfish/salmon carcasses are gold)
- Soak for 15-30 min then haul fast — Dungeness will let go if they sense the net rising
- Maximum 10 hoop nets per person (CA regs)

## Hotline / inseason changes

- **CDPH Marine Biotoxin Monitoring** is the canonical source for domoic-acid closures
- Check the CDPH advisory page before every crab trip; closures can be region-specific (Humboldt vs. Mendocino vs. Del Norte)

## Launch suitability

- **Humboldt Bay (interior)** — primary spot, hoop nets in the channels
- **Mad River Slough** — possible, smaller catches
- **Trinidad** — uncommon for kayak crabbers; bay is the standard
