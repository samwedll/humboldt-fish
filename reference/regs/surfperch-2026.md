# Surfperch — California Sport Fishing 2026

**Last updated:** 2026-05-17 (DRAFT — verify with CDFW before relying on)
**Region:** Northern CA — Humboldt County beaches, slough, and bay interior
**Source for verification:** <https://wildlife.ca.gov/Fishing/Ocean/Regulations/Surf-Fishing>

---

## Season

Open year-round in California surf, bay, and tidal estuary waters.

Surfperch species include: barred surfperch, redtail surfperch, walleye surfperch, calico surfperch, silver surfperch, pile perch, white seaperch, rubberlip seaperch, black perch.

## Bag limits

- **Daily bag:** 20 fish total in the surfperch family
- **Redtail surfperch sub-limit:** Most-targeted of the group, conservation-watch species. Verify current sub-limit at CDFW (historically capped at lower count of the daily 20).
- **Possession:** 2x daily bag (40 fish)

## Size limits

- Most species: no minimum length
- Redtail surfperch: verify current minimum size with CDFW (commonly 10.5" historically — may have changed)

## License and stamps

- **Required:** California Sport Fishing License (age 16+)
- **No ocean enhancement stamp required** (Humboldt is north of Pt. Arguello — that stamp is South Coast only)
- **No surfperch report card or surfperch validation**

## Gear and technique

- No special gear restrictions
- Barbed hooks allowed
- Pile worms, sand crabs, mussels, ghost shrimp work as bait
- Standard 7-9' surf rod with pyramid sinker

## Hotline / inseason changes

- CDFW does not run a dedicated surfperch hotline — check the CDFW website if conservation concerns trigger an emergency closure.

## Launch suitability

- **Trinidad State Beach** — beach surfperch fishing OK
- **Mad River Slough** — perch + jacksmelt in the slough channel
- **Humboldt Bay (Samoa, Fields, Woodley)** — surfperch around bay structure
- **Lagoons** — not the target species here (lagoons are trout/cutthroat water)
