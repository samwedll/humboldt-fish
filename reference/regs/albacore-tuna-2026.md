# Albacore Tuna — California 2026

**Last updated:** 2026-05-17 (DRAFT — verify with CDFW + NMFS before relying on)
**Region:** California offshore waters; the user's only legal launch is Trinidad
**Source for verification:** <https://wildlife.ca.gov/Fishing/Ocean/Regulations/Tuna>

---

## Season

- **Year-round** federal recreational; in California waters, no closed season

Real-world season (for Humboldt offshore from a kayak) is much narrower: **June – October** when warm water (60°F+) pushes inshore. Some years albacore stay 25-40 miles offshore — **out of kayak range**. Other years (often warm-water/El Niño-influenced summers), they push inside 5-10 miles, which is just within reach from Trinidad.

## Bag limits

- **Daily bag:** No limit on albacore tuna in California recreational
- **Possession:** No limit historically; check current CA regs

(Note: bluefin tuna and yellowfin tuna have separate, stricter regulations and federal HMS reporting requirements. This file is albacore-specific.)

## Size limits

- **None** for albacore

## License and stamps

- **Required:** CDFW Sport Fishing License (16+)
- **No special tuna stamp** for recreational albacore
- **HMS permit** is required for commercial; not for recreational

## Gear and technique

- Trolling cedar plugs, chrome jets, or feathers at 6-8 knots
- Ice on board — albacore must be bled and chilled immediately or quality drops fast
- **From a Hobie PA-14:** physically possible at 5-8 mph trolling, but a long offshore trip requires:
  - Calm seas + low swell for 6-8 hours
  - GPS + VHF + EPIRB
  - Float plan with detailed track
  - Not solo

## Hotline / inseason changes

- **NMFS West Coast HMS announcements:** <https://www.fisheries.noaa.gov/west-coast/sustainable-fisheries/west-coast-highly-migratory-species>
- Water temperature is the key signal — check satellite SST charts (e.g., Terrafin, FishTrack) before committing to an offshore trip
- Fishingthenorthcoast.com posts albacore reports when they're in

## Launch suitability

- **Trinidad** — only realistic launch (per personal Trinidad-only open-Pacific rule)
- **Range from Trinidad:** typically need to pedal 5-15 miles offshore; only viable when water temp pushes into the inner zone

## Trip considerations (user-specific)

- **Situational viability.** Most years albacore are too far offshore for a kayak. Only attempt with: water temp confirmation 60°F+ within paddle range, calm seas forecast for 6-8 hours, accompanied trip, full safety gear.
- **Not solo year 1** (or any year, probably — open Pacific kayak tuna fishing is a committed-paddler activity).
- The verdict layer should treat tuna days as a higher bar: GO only when conditions are deeply green AND a water-temp signal supports it. The water-temp signal is not yet wired into this tool; manual verify via SST charts.
