# Coastal Cutthroat Trout — Humboldt Lagoons 2026

**Last updated:** 2026-05-17 (DRAFT — verify with CDFW before relying on)
**Region:** Big Lagoon and Stone Lagoon (Humboldt Lagoons State Park)

## Season

**Big Lagoon:**
- Open year-round historically, with artificial-lure / barbless restrictions on certain seasons
- Verify 2026 dates and restrictions with CDFW District 1 regs

**Stone Lagoon:**
- Closed for spawning protection: **Nov 21 – last day of February** (historically)
- Open the rest of the year

Both are part of CDFW's coastal-cutthroat conservation strategy. **In-season closures and slot limits are not unusual** — verify before each trip.

## Bag limits

- **Daily bag:** 1 fish (Big Lagoon and Stone Lagoon are both 1-fish waters historically)
- **Possession:** 1 fish

## Size limits

- Verify current minimum size with CDFW (historically 14" minimum at Big Lagoon)

## Gear

- **Big Lagoon:** Artificial-lure-only with single-point barbless hook (no bait, no treble hooks) — historically. Verify 2026.
- **Stone Lagoon:** Same gear restrictions historically. Verify 2026.

## License

- **Required:** California Sport Fishing License (age 16+)
- **No salmon report card required** for cutthroat (only relevant to anadromous-salmon fishing)
- **No ocean enhancement stamp** (these are coastal freshwater — though brackish)

## Hotline / inseason changes

- No dedicated trout hotline. Check the CDFW website and Humboldt Lagoons State Park page before trips, especially in fall and spring around spawning.

## Launch suitability

- **Big Lagoon** — Big Lagoon County Park paved ramp; brackish, sheltered
- **Stone Lagoon** — primary trout target lagoon
- **Freshwater Lagoon** — limited cutthroat presence; mostly bluegill/bass per launches.md

## Conservation notes

Coastal cutthroat trout (Oncorhynchus clarkii clarkii) are a species of conservation concern in California. Catch-and-release is encouraged even when retention is legal. Use single barbless hooks, land quickly, keep the fish wet, support it head-into-current until it swims off on its own.
