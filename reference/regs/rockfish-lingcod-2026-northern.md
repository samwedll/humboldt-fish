# 2026 Recreational Rockfish & Lingcod — California Northern Management Area

**Zone:** CA-OR border (42°00' N) to 40°10' N latitude (just south of Cape Mendocino)
**Applies to:** Crescent City, Trinidad, Humboldt Bay, Eureka, and waters off most of Humboldt County
**Note on boundary:** If you fish south of 40°10' N (e.g., off Shelter Cove or Mendocino), you're in the **Mendocino Management Area** — same season structure in 2026 but **different vermilion sub-limit** (see below)
**Last updated:** May 2, 2026
**Source:** CDFW Groundfish Summary page (updated January 6, 2026); CCR T-14 §1.91, §27.25, §28.27, §28.28, §28.29, §28.55

## RCG Sub-Bag Limits (Northern Management Area)

The RCG complex (Rockfish + Cabezon + Greenlings) is 10 fish total. Within that 10:

| Species | Sub-limit |
|---|---|
| **Copper rockfish** | 1 fish |
| **Vermilion + sunset rockfish** (combined) | **4 fish** in Northern Mgmt Area only — **2 fish in all other CA areas** |
| **Canary rockfish** | 2 fish |

⚠️ The 4-fish vermilion limit is unique to the Northern Management Area. If you launch from Shelter Cove or any port south of 40°10' N, the vermilion limit drops to 2.

---

## PROHIBITED Species — No Retention at Any Time

These are zero-bag, year-round, all depths, all areas. Must be released — use a descending device.

- **Bronzespotted rockfish**
- **Cowcod**
- **Quillback rockfish**
- **Yelloweye rockfish**

(CCR T-14, §28.55)

## Species Categories (matters for closure rules)

### Nearshore Rockfish (CCR T-14, §1.91(a)(1))
Black, black-and-yellow, blue, brown, calico, China, copper, gopher, grass, kelp, olive rockfishes; treefish

### Shelf Rockfish (CCR T-14, §1.91(a)(3))
Bocaccio; canary, chilipepper, dusky, flag, greenblotched, greenspotted, greenstriped, harlequin, honeycomb, Mexican, pink, redstripe, rosethorn, rosy, shortbelly, silvergray, speckled, squarespot, starry, stripetail, tiger, vermilion/sunset, widow, yellowtail rockfishes

### Slope Rockfish (CCR T-14, §1.91(a)(4))
Aurora, bank, blackgill, darkblotched, redbanded, rougheye, sharpchin, shortraker, splitnose, yellowmouth rockfishes; Pacific ocean perch

### Cabezon and Greenlings (CCR T-14, §28.28, §28.29)
Counted in the RCG aggregate.

**Why this matters:** In other management areas, depth/season restrictions sometimes split nearshore from shelf/slope. In the Northern Management Area for 2026, **all categories are open all depths April 1 – December 31** — so the categorization mostly affects identification for sub-bag limits, not where you can fish.

## Descending Device Requirement (this is the gotcha)

You **must have a descending device on board and immediately available** when fishing for federal groundfish from any boat, including a kayak (CCR T-14, §27.20(b)(2)).

- A descending device sends a barotrauma-affected rockfish back to capture depth so it can recover
- Common types: SeaQualizer, Shelton Fish Descender, weighted milk crate
- **Wardens check for this** — getting caught without one is a citation
- Required even if you're targeting other species (lingcod, etc.) and groundfish are caught incidentally

## Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) — Check Before Fishing

The Humboldt coast has several MPAs where take is restricted or prohibited:

- **South Cape Mendocino State Marine Reserve** (no take)
- **Mattole Canyon State Marine Reserve** (no take)
- **Sea Lion Gulch State Marine Reserve** (no take)
- **Big Flat State Marine Conservation Area** (limited take)
- **Reading Rock State Marine Reserve / Conservation Area** (off Trinidad — partial closure)
- **Samoa State Marine Conservation Area** (in Humboldt Bay area)
- **South Humboldt Bay State Marine Recreational Management Area**

**Always check the CDFW Ocean Sport Fishing Interactive Map before fishing a new spot.** MPAs are GPS-defined and the lines aren't visible on the water.

## Filleting at Sea Rules

You **may** fillet aboard a vessel, BUT:

- **Lingcod fillets:** min 14" length, entire skin attached
- **Rockfish fillets:** no min length, entire skin attached
- **Cabezon/greenling fillets:** entire skin attached, no min length
- **California scorpionfish (sculpin):** any size, skin attached
- A "fillet" = flesh from one side of fish from head to tail, removed in a single continuous piece (CCR T-14, §27.65(a))

This matters more for charter boats than kayaks — most kayak anglers ice whole fish and fillet at home — but if you do fillet on the water, follow the rules.

## Practical Notes for Kayak Fishing from Humboldt

- The Northern Management Area's **all-depths-April-through-December** structure is the most generous in the state. No 20-fathom line to worry about, no 50-fathom line.
- Black rockfish are the most common kayak target — abundant inshore around Trinidad Head, Patrick's Point, the rocks off Big Lagoon.
- Lingcod hang in the same rocky areas. Live bait (small black rockfish or sand dabs) on a dropper rig works well.
- **Cape Mendocino** is the ID-required boundary fish. If you launch from Shelter Cove and head north, you cross into the Northern Mgmt Area (more vermilion allowed). If you head south, you stay in Mendocino Mgmt Area (2 vermilion). For most kayak ranges this is academic.
- **Crab combo trips** are easy here — the rockfish season being open all year (April–Dec) overlaps crab season fully, unlike further south.
