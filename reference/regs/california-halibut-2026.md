# California Halibut — Northern District 2026

**Last updated:** 2026-05-17 (DRAFT — verify with CDFW before relying on)
**Region:** Humboldt Bay interior, Mad River Slough, estuaries
**Source for verification:** <https://wildlife.ca.gov/Fishing/Ocean/Regulations/Saltwater-General>

---

## Season

Open year-round in California marine waters. Most active in Humboldt Bay during the warmer months (May–October).

## Bag limits

- **Daily bag:** 3 fish
- **Possession:** 6 fish (2× daily)

## Size limits

- **Minimum:** 22 inches total length

## License and stamps

- **Required:** CDFW Sport Fishing License (16+)
- **No ocean enhancement stamp required** (Humboldt is north of Pt. Arguello)
- **No halibut report card** for California halibut (that card is Pacific halibut only)

## Gear and technique

- Drift fishing with live bait (anchovies, smelt) at slack-to-flooding tide
- Plastic swimbaits over sandy bottoms in 10-30 ft depth
- Halibut spreader rig with 8-12 oz weight
- Net to land — halibut shake hooks at the surface

## Hotline / inseason changes

- No dedicated CA halibut hotline. Domoic-acid closures occasionally affect bay shellfish but not halibut directly.

## Launch suitability

- **Humboldt Bay (interior)** — primary spot, channels and sandy flats
- **Mad River Slough** — occasional larger fish at the river mouth
- **Trinidad** — not a target species there (Pacific halibut is the deeper-water analog)
