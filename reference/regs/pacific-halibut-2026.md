# Pacific Halibut — California 2026

**Last updated:** 2026-05-17 (DRAFT — verify with CDFW + IPHC before relying on)
**Region:** Open Pacific north of Pt. Arena; the user's only legal launch is Trinidad
**Sources for verification:**
- CDFW: <https://wildlife.ca.gov/Fishing/Ocean/Regulations/Pacific-Halibut>
- IPHC: <https://www.iphc.int/management/sport-fishing-regulations>

## Season

- **Typical window:** May 1 – November 15 (verify annually — IPHC sets opening based on quota)
- The season **closes inseason without warning** when the California recreational quota is met. This routinely happens by mid-summer.

## Bag limits

- **Daily bag:** 1 fish per angler
- **Possession:** 2 fish

## Size limits

- **Minimum:** 22 inches total length (verify 2026)

## License and stamps

- **Required:** CDFW Sport Fishing License (16+)
- **Required:** Pacific Halibut Card (free, online from CDFW) — must be carried, must record each fish kept immediately upon retention
- **No ocean enhancement stamp** required for Humboldt

## Gear and technique

- Deep-water bottom fishing on flat sand bottoms, typically 80-200 ft offshore of Trinidad
- Heavy jigging rod with 24-32 oz lead-head jig + curly tail OR meat-baited circle hook on dropper rig
- Large gaff or BogaGrip — halibut up to 100+ lb possible
- **From a Hobie PA-14:** doable but long pedal/paddle to productive depth (typically 3-5 miles offshore). Plan a 6-8 hour trip with backup.

## Hotline / inseason changes

- **Inseason closure announcements:** check CDFW Pacific Halibut page; usually a press release when quota is approached
- IPHC also posts updates: <https://www.iphc.int/management/sport-fishing-regulations>
- **Call within 24h of launch** — closures can drop fast in late summer

## Launch suitability

- **Trinidad** — only realistic launch for the user (per personal Trinidad-only open-Pacific rule)
- Pacific halibut hold over sand at 80-200 ft; from Trinidad Indian Beach this is 3-5 miles offshore on a heading of W to WSW

## Trip considerations (user-specific)

- **Not solo year 1.** Open-Pacific, deep water, long return trip.
- **Long forecast horizon needed:** wind/swell forecast must hold for 6-8 hours, not just the morning launch window
- **Heavy load on the kayak:** halibut over 30 lb is hard to land alone and changes the boat's balance significantly
- **Bring a marine VHF and float plan.** Notify the designated shore contact of departure + ETA + check-in schedule.
